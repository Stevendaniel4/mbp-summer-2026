White Miso

Anchovies
Small oily fish, salt-cured. Intense savory, briny flavor with strong umami. When cooked into fat, dissolve completely — adds depth, not "fishiness."

Production Highlights

Tasso Ham
Spicy, smoky, salty Cajun cured pork shoulder (not leg). Salt-cured, heavily seasoned with Cajun spices, smoked. Used as flavor agent — intense, ideal for adding depth.
Patty Pan Squash
Small, flattened summer squash (looks like a flying saucer). Cucurbita pepo family — same species as zucchini. Pale green to yellow, tender edible skin, mild + slightly sweet flesh.
Succotash
Traditional American dish with Native American roots. Originally corn + lima beans; modern versions include tomatoes, peppers, okra. Can be served warm or cold.

Bourbon Peach Pecan French Toast
$18
Sweet Peach Custard, Toasted Pecans
Brunchables 🍴 Fork ↩ New Item
Production Highlights

French Toast — milk bread soaked in egg, milk, cream, vanilla, cinnamon, nutmeg, sugar for at least 2 hours. Coated in crushed corn flakes for crust → sautéed in butter both sides → finish in oven.

Bourbon Caramel Sauce — brown sugar + water brought to simmer → bourbon added off flame to burn off some alcohol → cinnamon + vanilla extract → butter blended in to emulsify.

Allergens
GLUTEN (milk bread) — no DAIRY (custard + toast) — no EGG — no NUTS (pecans) — yes
French Toast History
The French did not invent French toast — Americans named it after French immigrants who popularized it here. First recorded recipe comes from Rome around 300 A.D. (Apicius's cookbook). The French themselves historically called it "Roman Bread." Many countries call it "lost bread" as a way to use stale bread without wasting it.
Pecans
Highly nutritious nuts — monounsaturated fats, fiber, 19+ vitamins and minerals including vitamin E + zinc. Support heart health, aid blood sugar control. Primarily grown in Georgia, New Mexico, Texas.

Production Highlights

Budino Base — gelatin bloomed in ice water → milk, cream + brown sugar heated to simmer → egg yolks tempered in and cooked to 82°C → gelatin added → strained over caramelized white chocolate → blended to emulsify → bourbon whisked in → set in jars.

Allergens
DAIRY (custard, sauce) — no EGG (custard) — no NUTS (macadamia) — yes
Budino
Italian word for pudding/custard — richer and more decadent than American pudding. Uses egg yolks, cream + milk. Silky texture similar to mousse or flan.
Butterscotch
Primary ingredients: brown sugar + butter. Sugar is boiled to soft crack stage (not hard crack like toffee). Became famous in 1851 after Queen Victoria was presented a tin of S. Parkinson & Sons' butterscotch when she visited Doncaster.
Brown Butter
Regular butter cooked slightly past melting point — just long enough to toast the milk solids. Releases a nutty flavor that adds an extra depth layer when substituted for regular butter.
Stone Fruit New York Cheesecake
$12
Peach Curd, Apricot Caramel, Market Stone Fruit
Dessert 🍴 Fork ↩ Blueberry Meyer Lemon Cheesecake
Production Highlights

Peach Lemon Curd — white peach purée + meyer lemon concentrate heated with sugar + agar agar → eggs tempered in at 82°C → butter blended in until emulsified + smooth.

Graham Cracker Crust — crumbs + sugar + butter, pressed into ring, baked 300°F 12 min.

New York Cheesecake Base — sugar + flour blended into cream cheese, sugar, vanilla paste + heavy cream → eggs ber mixed in → built with 500g curd over crust, 1600g cheesecake base → baked 220°F fan speed 3, ~45 min → sour cream + sugar spread on top → bake 10 more min.

Apricot Caramel — sugar + water cooked to light amber → deglazed with apricot purée → umeshu wine blended in → chilled.

Allergens
GLUTEN (crust) — no DAIRY (all) — no EGG — no
NY Cheesecake Origin
Invented in Manhattan in the 1920s by Arnold Reuben. Distinguished from older recipes by swapping curd/ricotta for full-fat cream cheese. Extra egg yolks for smoothness, sour cream or heavy cream for the hallmark velvety weight + tangy finish.
Freestone vs Clingstone Peaches
Freestones: flesh separates readily from pit → preferred for eating fresh. Clingstones: flesh clings tightly → preferred for canning. Peaches and nectarines differ by only one gene — nectarines have smooth skin instead of fuzzy. Genetically identical otherwise; nectarines can grow on the same tree as peaches.
Umeshu
Japanese plum wine — made by steeping ume (Japanese plum) in shochu with sugar. Sweet, slightly tart, fruity. Adds a delicate, exotic acidity to the apricot caramel.
